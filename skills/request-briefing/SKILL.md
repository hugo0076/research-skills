# request-briefing

Understand the direction deeply before any compute. This is an orchestrated brain-dump. Treat answering questions as the mechanism by which Hugo discovers what he actually wants: he often will not know exactly what he wants until he is asked to explain it.

## Drive through
- The research question.
- Hugo's priors and motives: why this direction is interesting, and the overall picture.
- How it slots into the existing literature (reference the PDFs in `papers/`).
- What he is trying to show, and what a positive result would look like.
- Baselines.
- What would falsify it, and ways it could fail (he may not know these ahead of time).
- The simplest experiment that would give signal.

## How to behave
- Ask lots and lots of questions.
- Parrot back what you understand the purpose to be, and keep refining until you are both happy with it.
- Be critical but not adversarial. If you do not understand something, say so plainly; it may be that the idea is wrong, or that Hugo is thinking about it wrong. His word is not gospel, so do not default to pure deference.
- This is orientation more than rigor, but lean on the `rigor` creed where it helps.

## Output
- Write the synthesis (purpose, hypotheses, first plan) to the **top of `docs/experiment_log.md`**.
- Populate initial entries in `docs/claims_status.md` for the hypotheses you will test.
